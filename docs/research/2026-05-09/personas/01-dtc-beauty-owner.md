# Persona 01 — DTC beauty owner, Bangkok

## Identity

**Name:** Nattaya "Nat" Phromsri
**Age:** 31
**Role:** Founder & CEO
**Education:** BBA, Chulalongkorn; one year doing marketing at L'Oréal Thailand before quitting to start her own brand.

## Company snapshot

**Brand:** "Klin Skin" — clean-formulation skincare (cleansing balm, exfoliant toner, two serums, sunscreen). DTC-only, no retail.
**Location:** Bangkok (office in Ari, fulfillment in Nonthaburi).
**Headcount:** 8 (Nat + co-founder + 2 marketing + 2 customer support + 2 fulfillment).
**Monthly revenue:** ~3.2M THB (~$92k USD), 30% MoM growth in last 6 months from a viral TikTok.
**Channels (by % of orders):** LINE OA Shop 42%, Shopee 28%, TikTok Shop 18%, Instagram DM-to-checkout 8%, own website 4%.
**Conversation volume:** ~600 inbound DMs/day across all channels, peaks to 1,400 on payday + livestream nights.

## Current stack

- **LINE OA** with LINE Shopping (in-LINE checkout) — set up by an agency 8 months ago, costs 8,500฿/mo for the Manychat-equivalent flows.
- **Manychat** for Instagram + Facebook DM auto-replies. Paid plan ~$45/mo.
- **Shopee Chat** + Shopee Seller Centre — 2 staff toggle between this and LINE all day.
- **TikTok Shop Seller Center** — desktop only, the support staff hate it.
- **Google Sheets** master CRM. ~14 columns. Manually updated. Already broken.
- **Shipnity** for fulfillment, **Omise** + **2C2P** + LINE Pay for payments.

## Day-in-the-life pain (Nat's words, condensed)

> "Every morning I open four tabs — LINE Official Account Manager, Shopee Seller Centre, TikTok Shop, and our Sheet. By 11am I've answered 'มีโค้ดส่วนลดไหมคะ' twenty times in three different windows. The annoying thing is, three of those twenty are the same customer trying us on different platforms because she didn't get a reply fast enough on the first one. We lose her trust before we lose the sale.
>
> The Manychat flow on LINE was supposed to fix this. It does, kind of, until someone asks something off-script. Then it just falls back to 'รอแอดมินตอบนะคะ' and the customer waits 40 minutes. Our reply-time SLA is supposed to be 5 minutes. We hit it maybe 30% of the time outside business hours.
>
> What kills me is I *know* we're losing money. Every Friday I export the Shopee chat log and find 8–12 conversations where someone asked about an ingredient or shipping window and nobody replied for 6+ hours. That's at least 4 lost orders. 600฿ AOV. Every week."

## Budget authority

- **Decision-maker:** Nat herself, no committee.
- **Comfort zone:** 3,000–8,000 THB/month for a tool. Will go to 15,000 if it visibly saves a CS hire.
- **Procurement style:** Trial-led. If she can sign up with credit card and see value in 7 days, she'll keep paying. Hates sales calls.
- **Hard ceiling without ROI proof:** 25,000 THB/month.

## Decision criteria (ranked)

1. **Time-to-value under 1 week.** Has been burned by 3-month onboarding from a "real CRM" sales rep last year.
2. **Speaks Thai well.** Including slang, sticker-replies, and the "555" / "ค่ะ/คะ" register correctness.
3. **LINE OA first-class.** Anything that treats LINE as an afterthought (most Western SaaS) is dead on arrival.
4. **Doesn't replace her brain on brand voice.** She has rejected Manychat templates that "sound like a bank."
5. **Plays nice with Shipnity, Omise, LINE Pay.** No re-platforming.

## Default objections (the things she reaches for first)

- *"AI ตอบไม่เหมือนคนคะ ลูกค้าจับได้"* — "AI replies don't sound human, customers can tell, it hurts the brand."
- *"แล้วถ้ามันตอบผิดล่ะ?"* — "What if it answers wrong? Skincare with the wrong claim is a regulator problem."
- *"ราคาเท่าไรนะคะ?"* — Always asks price within the first 2 minutes. Will quietly leave the call if it sounds enterprise.
- *"แค่ LINE ใช้ได้ไหม?"* — "Can it just do LINE? I don't want to learn a whole new system."
- *"ของไทยมีคนช่วยตั้งให้ไหม?"* — "Is there a Thai-speaking person who'll set it up?" (Implementation hand-holding matters more than docs.)

## Speech style for roleplay

Code-switches Thai/English fluidly, more Thai when stressed, more English when explaining strategy. Casual "คะ" particles. Uses "OA" for LINE Official Account, "ลูกค้า" for customer. Will say things like "honestly nah, that's overkill for us" and "okay but show me, not tell me." Suspicious of anything that sounds like a deck. Warm but blunt.

## What she'd pay extra for (hypothesis going in)

A single inbox where her two CS staff could see LINE + Shopee + TikTok in one window with AI drafts she could approve in batch from her phone at 10pm. The "drafts I approve from phone" framing is what would make her take out her credit card.
