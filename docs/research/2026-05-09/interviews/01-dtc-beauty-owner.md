## Q4. What did you try before that didn't work?

>
> Then I tried Manychat properly — Pro plan, $45 a month. It does Instagram and Facebook fine. LINE it kind of does, but the templates feel like a robot. Customers reply 'พี่เป็นคนหรือเปล่าคะ' which is exactly the question you don't want them asking. We turned off the LINE side after two weeks.
>

## Q5. What would make tomorrow 50% easier?

> "Easy. One inbox. One screen. Every channel in it — LINE, Shopee, TikTok, IG, FB. I see who the customer is, what they bought before, what they asked last time. I don't care if it's pretty. I care that I stop toggling.
>
> Second thing — drafts. I don't want full auto-reply, I've been burned. I want the AI to *write the reply* for the easy stuff — code questions, shipping windows, ingredient lists — and I tap approve from my phone. Like, I'm at dinner, I see 30 drafts queued, I swipe through, approve 25, edit 4, escalate 1. That's the thing. If I could do that at 10pm in bed, my Pim and Mook get their evenings back and I sleep better.
>
> 50% easier? Honestly even just the inbox alone, no AI, would save us probably 3 hours a day across the team. Add the draft thing and we'd close the SLA gap from 30% to like 80%, which is real money. ลูกค้าไม่หนี.
>
> What I *don't* need: more dashboards. I have enough dashboards. Don't give me a new dashboard and call it innovation, please."

Times 4 weeks, that's nearly 10,000 a month, only counting Shopee, only counting the ones I can see.
>
> LINE I can't even export cleanly so I'm guessing — probably 2x that. TikTok? ใครจะรู้. Total estimate, lost-orders alone, 30,000–40,000 baht a month from slow replies. That's a CS hire. That's a real number.
>
> Then the soft cost — Pim almost quit in March. If I have to recruit and train another CS person it's 25,000 baht in lost productivity easy, plus the risk that the next one quits too because the tools are still bad.
>

>
> So combined, I'm losing — call it 50,000 baht a month minimum from this exact problem. ที่จริงน่าจะมากกว่านั้น."

## Q14. What didn't I ask about that matters more?

> "You didn't ask me about *handoff*. The hardest moment in customer ops isn't the AI replying or the human replying — it's the *transition* between them. Right now when Manychat gives up and says 'รอแอดมินตอบนะคะ,' the customer waits 40 minutes and we lose them. The handoff is silent and broken.
>
> Better question: when the AI escalates, does the human get a *summary* of what's happened, the customer's history, the suggested reply, and a reason for escalation — all in one card? >
